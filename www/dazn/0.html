DAZN

DAZN (/dəˈzoʊn/; pronounced "da zone") is a British over-the-top sports streaming and entertainment platform. Founded in 2007 as Perform Group via the merger of Premium TV Limited and Inform Group, it is owned by Access Industries, the investment group founded by Sir Len Blavatnik, and is headquartered in London, England. Shay Segev is DAZN's CEO as of January 2021. The non-executive directors are Lincoln Benet, John Gleasure and Guillaume D’Hauteville. The DAZN platform was founded in 2015 and broadcasts live and on-demand sport in over 200 countries worldwide with a strong domestic presence in Italy, Spain, Germany, Japan, France, Portugal, Belgium, Taiwan, the United States and Canada, where it has key domestic broadcast rights. It is considered to be Europe's largest digital sports broadcaster with over 75 programming rights. As of 2023, the service has 20 million paid subscribers globally.

Streaming Technology and Infrastructure

DAZN operates on a foundation of Dynamic Adaptive Streaming over HTTP (DASH), which is more than just a network protocol; it represents a paradigm for linking multimedia resources—specifically video and audio—with the temporal context of their delivery. DASH is designed to enhance the user experience by optimizing video playback quality through dynamic bitrate adjustments, responding to varying network conditions and device capabilities. Research has shown that DASH has become a leading standard in over-the-top (OTT) streaming due to its capability to adapt efficiently to fluctuating network environments, minimizing buffering and ensuring a seamless viewing experience (Chen et al., 2022). The methodology of DASH involves segmenting video content into smaller parts, each encoded at multiple bitrates, enabling the client to select the highest quality stream available without interruptions. This approach is crucial for enhancing user satisfaction and optimizing network performance in the delivery of live and on-demand content (Park et al., 2023).

To deliver content efficiently, DAZN relies on a robust network of Content Delivery Network (CDN) infrastructures. In Italy, DAZN partners with leading CDN providers, including Akamai, Amazon CloudFront, Fastly, and Video Streaming. This multi-CDN strategy allows DAZN to leverage the strengths of each provider, ensuring that users are connected to the nearest server based on their geographic location. This deployment minimizes latency and enhances overall streaming performance, resulting in a smoother viewing experience for customers throughout the country.

The localization of servers used for multimedia content distribution is pivotal in shaping the domain naming conventions of these servers. Each CDN provider, such as Akamai or Amazon CloudFront, adopts distinct naming practices. For example, servers managed by Akamai typically include specific keywords associated with the provider in their domains, such as *livedazn.akamaized.net, in case of Amakai. Similarly, Amazon CloudFront servers may exhibit different naming characteristics, as seen in *ac-cdn.live.indazn.com. More over, for all the HTTP servers observed in the Video Streaming infrastructure, the naming *livedazn.daznedge.net has been observed to be fairly frequent.
